Award-Winning Sites' Analysis

Welcome! This is a small analysis of four different award-winning websites that I find interesting in one way or another.

JIII ATELIER

Click here to go to the website

1. Introduction

The website of JIII ATELIER is a minimalistic portfolio website created by Yumi Nakaji for a production office that goes by the name of JIII ATELIER.

This site showcases what the website is, the studio's portfolio, but also, it promotes the services that the studio offers to its clients.

Top of the website's home page

2. First Impressions

The first thing that made me take a look at this website was its color palette. I am usually very much attracted to anything that uses pastel colors, but in this website, it mixes it with a gradient, which I am not always a big fan of. However, in this case, the overall look of the website manages to bring me a strong calming feeling, which accentuates the desire to interact more with the website.

Work page with interactions when hovering

3. Architecture Analysis

The JIII ATELIER website is more formal than casual multiple sections and pages type of website, as the home page can be slowly scrolled to discover the rest of the content on the page. Which brings us to the menu, on the top right corner, the website provides the user a menu with the shape of a star inside a square, which opens up the different pages that are able to visit inside the website.

Regarding its cohesiveness, the website offers on the home page its main services, while also showcasing some of their previous work, while on the menu, the order varies a little bit, and after the home page, the most important page (following the hierarchy that is on the website) is the “about” page. This can be a little confusing, but it doesn’t affect the easy access that the user has to finding the different parts of the website.

Later on, we can also see some interesting things along the page, such as horizontal navigation where their services are provided. This is shown in rectangular UX cards, that are situated one behind the other in a horizontal carousel, which works by scrolling, or by clicking the pagination icons shown below those same UX cards. Besides this, there is also a section where instead of scrolling downwards, the elements come up while scrolling, but without the page going on on its length, much as if the screen was freezed, but not the elements of the page.

Moreover, the textual content of the website doesn’t contain any mistakes. However, it is kind of brief, making it harder to understand some of the content, as the website itself doesn’t have any type of storytelling that could give the user more clues of the paragraphs.

Website's menu





Service section on the home page, where the 3D object is animated

4. Visual Analysis

As it was mentioned before, the color palette uses different pastel colors, more specifically: white, pink, blue and purple, bringing out a softer approach of the brand.

For the typography, it mainly uses three. Two sans serif ones, which are Satoshi, used for most of the text and Noto Sans JP, used only for the text that is in Japanese; and a serif one that is Gall, which is used as an accent, for those words that he wants to give more importance.

Furthermore, the image style is mostly 3D illustration, which doesn’t really correlate with the way they are promoting the studio’s services. And also, there are some sections where the fixed elements overlap with the text, making it hard to read, Nevertheless, the overall experience of the website makes up for it, with little animations and a good layout that doesn’t look too overdone, but with enough space to let the user breathe through their scroll.

UX cards that work with horizontal scrolling

5. Interaction

When it comes to interaction, it is mostly easy and fun to scroll through the home page, as it has many 3D animations that makes the overall page much more immersive. For starters, the first part of the home page uses their logo as a 3D element that moves backwards the moment the scrolling starts, until one point, where it comes back again, until it disappears out of the viewport.

Also, there is a creative touch to the menu and the rectangular UX cards, as the cursor changes to a large, pixelated arrow, when in contact with the menu icon or the UX cards part. As well, when there are buttons for the user to look up more information about that section, the buttons change from a plus icon to a little star, and the text buttons have a tiny animation in their letters, in the same way as the arcade machines, where the different elements turn 360º one right after the other.

Footer of the home page with a little text animation

6. Usability & Accessibility

In general, it is easy to access the different parts of the website, as there isn’t much content for the user to get lost at some point. Nevertheless, the big UX cards in the “works” section do make it difficult to click on each card, as the button is very visible and it changes from a plus sign to a star, making it unclear for the user to know exactly whether they should click on the button or just anywhere on the UX card.

Regarding the loading times, the website doesn’t take much time to, but it could also be hidden with the text animations that are happening in the meantime. Also, it is adapted to smaller devices, by changing the size of the bigger UX cards, and the amount of elements that can be added to the viewport.

Loader that appears when entering the website

7. Excellence Criteria & Conclusion

CATEGORY CRITERIA
DESIGN 3/5☆ The design gives a sense of cleanliness and relaxation with its minimalistic style, and the colors also give a feeling of a magic and sweet dream, making the first impression very enjoyable.
USABILITY 3/5☆ The website is easy to understand and to navigate through, without many complications or elements that can cause misunderstandings to the user.
CREATIVITY 3/5☆ Even though most of their animations and elements aren’t very innovative, I also feel as if the way they have used them brings a new perspective of how to use these types of things.
CONTENT 1/5☆ The user is able to see what the main point of the website is, but the use of only Japanese in some parts, and the little pieces of information can create some doubts about the information.
CONCLUSION
2,5/5☆		 All in all, I believe that this website was aesthetically pleasing, and it worked well in general. It might have its little negative points, which may cause little mishaps on the user experience, but overall, it was easy and interesting.

PICTORIC

Click here to go to the website

1. Introduction

Founded in 2014, PICTORIC is a website made by a group of independent Ukrainian curators to build a creative circle for different illustrators and designers, make new projects come alive, and also, to organize exhibitions and workshops.

The main purpose of this website is not only to support illustrators and other creative people around the world, but to spread knowledge about Ukrainian culture and, to advertise the need of a higher importance of the visual language in the academic and social sector.

About us page, where the yellow frame moves when hovering

2. First Impressions

There’re actually two different elements that caught my eye at first: the illustration style and the color palette. I believe that the little star illustration feels very amicable, and mixed with the vibrant colors that they have used, the site gives a really kind and soft vibe.

Overall, the website looks visually appealing and interesting with a first quick scroll through its home page, as there are many illustrations that are able to catch the user’s attention.

Top section of the home page, with animations on the logo and the sun illustrations

3. Architecture Analysis

The PICTORIC website is a multiple paged website that has a casual tone to its whole concept, with the use of cute illustrations that follow on their own a very subtle and progressive storytelling, showing a general idea of the creative process, which matches really well the concept of the website.

The menu on the website is already exposed on the right side of the navigation bar, where it appears with a flower, which could be confused as an extension of the menu too. However, the home page is organised so that it shows all the different pages that the website has, and that are stated on the menu, making it easy for the user to find each section, as each part has its own “more info” button.

Besides, without the use of any special techniques, the navigation begins by showing some illustration projects, and ending with the possible subscription for their illustration contest. Making it cohesive by creating a common storyline throughout the whole navigation. This is achieved by showing pictures of already existing artworks, and with the help of larger texts that explain what everything is in a clear and uninterrupted way, and also, some key words scattered in some parts of the website that help you understand their concept.

Section of the home page with a button that's animated when hovering





2D illustration that rotates and goes down while scrolling

4. Visual Analysis

The website, which follows a cohesive layout with many interesting animations, is mainly composed by elements that use a vibrant yellow and blue, along with white and grey, to create a visual balance, and not to saturate the site with too much color, as the sections of the home page that showcase some illustrators’ works already contain too many colors, which can look overwhelming if it is overcombined with the blue and yellow used in the website’s color palette.

For the typography, the principal one is GT America LCGV, a sans serif typography that brings out more of the casual tone of the website, and is used all along the platform for big titles, subtitles, additional information and text bodies. However, we can also observe that their logo uses a more geometric type of typography, which kind of simulates paper cut letters, and on some animations and details they use a more calligraphic typography that matches the illustration style of the site.

On the other hand, as it was mentioned previously, the image (illustration) style that this site possesses is friendly and organic, with a cute vibe to it. And it is this image style that brings the visual side of the website to match with its main message, to bring illustrators around the world together.

Illustration with interactive bar





Section from the home page with animated buttons (circles with text)

5. Interaction

The interactivity of this website is truly entertaining since the start of the home page. At the very top, on the navigation bar we can find an animated logo, where its letters rotate 360º one after the other, and a menu that has hovering effects, along with a little flower illustration that not only rotates on its axis while the user scrolls through the length of the home page, but it shows an animated quote when it’s clicked on too.

Then, on the home page there are some other interesting interactions, as the page itself starts with a hero interaction. Starting with the hovering elements, there are two sections in which we can find some circles of different sizes, where the small ones may contain words or illustrations. Those circles move when hovered, and the ones at the top of the home page are even accessible, taking the user to a new page of the website. Additionally, in some sections, the cursor changes to a circumference that lets you drag the different elements in that part. Following that, there is a very compelling section, which is the one for their contest. On this part, the cursor will draw a thick line, which will slowly disappear, wherever it hovers, and this interaction is also applied to their boutique page, which is now empty.

The site may not use any sounds or 3D elements, but the combination of the existing interactions makes it creative and fun to see, as the user can play with many different parts of the website, instead of just scrolling through it.

Group of interactive illustrations that can be passed like real book pages





Section from the home page where the background is painted where the cursor hovers

6. Usability & Accessibility

The loading time is friendly, as the only moment that it takes “longer” to load is when the user first opens the website. Nevertheless, the loading time is clearly shown on the screen, making the wait more accessible and easy.

Regarding the user experience, the navigation follows a clear order that is easy to comprehend, without many things that can be truly confusing for the user. Except for the little flower icon on the navigation bar, which disappears on smaller devices where the responsiveness of the website changes the whole navigation bar and substitutes it for their logo and a hamburger menu icon.

Loader that appears when entering the website

7. Excellence Criteria & Conclusion

CATEGORY CRITERIA
DESIGN 4/5☆ The overall design is aesthetically pleasing, bringing the user a happy feeling with its style and lively colours.
USABILITY 4/5☆ The navigation doesn’t give the user any type of confusing directions, making the whole process clear and entertaining with the included interactions.
CREATIVITY 5/5☆ Even though its simplicity, the site has found a way to create a balance between the designated style of the website and the artworks that have been presented to their community, giving place to a highly creative, but not oversaturated space.
CONTENT 4/5☆ The elements that appear are really cohesive with their respective texts, directing the user through the website’s content in a logical and direct way, which makes the user understand from the very beginning what the main purpose of this site is.
CONCLUSION
4,25/5☆		 In a nutshell, I find this website really pleasing to scroll through because of its many microinteractions. Moreover, the style and purpose feels consistent throughout the totality of the site, which makes the whole experience much more enjoyable.

BOMBON

Click here to go to the website

1. Introduction

This is a website dedicated to a fake Swedish candy brand called BOMBON, which was built as inspiration for other product brands. Because of its long name “Bombon Royal Swedish Candy”, the whole branding, and therefore website, is inspired in a candy kingdom, where the main characters are the different types of candy that this brand sells.

Section of the home page that charges letters while scrolling down

2. First Impressions

I choose this website primarily because of how eye-catching its thumbnail is. With its vibrant colors and bubbly aesthetic it causes a soft feeling on the viewer. The castle and the different candies scattered on the floor, combined with the colors from the website’s color palette really takes the user into a trip down memory lane, reminding them about a sweet childhood.

Section from the home page with popup buttons and hovering animations

3. Architecture Analysis

The website of BOMBON is a multiple section website that, with a casual and close tone, chooses to immerse their clients into a magical storyline to sell their products.

The home page starts with a navigation bar that displays the brand’s logo and the two most important pages for a website that is dedicated to a product, their shop and the customer’s cart, along with an integrated menu that opens up the different pages that the website has, excluding the shopping cart.

From the navigation bar, the website already states the organisation of the website, showing that the shop is the main page, apart from the home page, where the user gets a summary of what this brand sells, and right after that, little UX cards of their products. This cohesive navigation is maintained throughout all the website, as the main purpose of the site is to sell their product.

Furthermore, along the navigation we can find the use of some special techniques such as horizontal navigation used for the section with the UX cards that depict their mixed products, or the element stacking that is used by the end of the home page, to show their previous customer’s opinions on their products. All of these, together with their descriptive texts really make a difference on the easy access to the different pages of this site. Nevertheless, these two interactions are included in the navigation flow of the home page, which can cause a little bit of confusion, as they are not scrollings that can be done separately.

Website's menu





UX card stacking animation when scrolling down

4. Visual Analysis

For their color palette, their main colors are purple, white, black and a greenish yellow, which works well because of the complementary state of the purple and yellow colors. Besides, this combination really brings out a more fluffy and magical, but mysterious vibe to the website, making it consistent with their branding.

Additionally, they use two sans serif typographies for their site. Akina is the one used for the titles, which are big and bold, bringing a lot of visual weight to the titles on this site. And Roundo is the typography used for the rest of the texts, because of its lighter weight and simpler shapes, it balances out the difference between these two typographies and how they work on the website.

On the other side, the imagery is mostly static 3D objects, more specifically their candy, which is used for the main part of the home page, and also for interactive UX cards. The use of these images matches the message of the website, as by showing the way that their product looks will lead customers to grow their faith for this brand and what it sells.

And lastly, the layout is structured in an organised way, but the great amount of elements, together with the large size that they have might make the website look kind of overdone in the long run.

Shop page with product UX cards





3D animated UX cards when hovering

5. Interaction

The first interactions that the user will find are a little game that can be opened by clicking on the yellow CTA button that appears at the top of the home page, and a music note icon on the bottom left corner of the viewport, where the user can decide whether they want to navigate the website with or without music.

These types of fun interactions continue the more we scroll, because the further we go, we don’t only just see some animations on the texts that appear on the website, but also, there are many buttons that can be clicked for animations to appear or carousels to move, and many other elements that change with a quick hover.

From that last part, there's an especially creative section that depicts a candy kingdom, where the user will be able to click on some buttons to see how this brand has used famous paintings to make their brand more fun. Even if it doesn’t really correlate with the brand’s image, it does give the website a more artistic point.

Interactive's section with opened popup

6. Usability & Accessibility

This Swedish candy website encompasses a series of elements that when combined make the user experience quite exciting, because the user cannot really know how the next section is going to work out. In addition, something that makes the user experience even more enjoyable is its short loading times, which quickly shows the user the pages that they want to navigate through. Still, there is also a negative side to the navigation through this website, as it was said before, their elements are abundant and large in size, not letting much space for the website to breath, which can make the navigation experience slightly heavy in some parts.

Nevertheless, it was mentioned before the amusing effect that their interactive imaginary causes to the user, and even though the website is adapted for smaller devices, the interactive imagery is forgotten on the mobile version, as it isn’t really possible to hover on a phone, but those that are just animations are still available even when the website is adapted so that it’s responsive for different-sized devices.

Website's shopping cart

7. Excellence Criteria & Conclusion

CATEGORY CRITERIA
DESIGN 2/5☆ The maximalist style of the website seems ingenious at first, but the large size of the titles and horizontal UX cards, can make it a little hard to scroll through for a long time.
USABILITY 4/5☆ All the elements are quick to locate, and because of its descriptive texts and visual icons, the user can effortlessly find the different pages and sections on this site.
CREATIVITY 4/5☆ The idea of creating a princess-like story just to sell some candy seems really unique.
CONTENT 4/5☆ The importance of what each page does inside this site is strongly marked, which helps the user get very rapidly the message of this website: to sell Sweedish candy.
CONCLUSION
3,5/5☆		 In brief, this website displays a creative and original way to sell a product, by creating a fantasy-inspired storyline that correlates with the brand’s name, and the great amount of products that they have.

ORAGE STUDIO

Click here to go to the website

1. Introduction

ORAGE is a portfolio website for the creative post production studio of Orage studio. In this website, they showcase the different works that they have done since 2021, and on top of that, they display the different services that the studio applies.

Furthermore, there are other things that can be done through this website, such as applying for a job in this studio, or finding all their different socials and contact forms.

Animated and aesthetically coordinated loader that appears when entering the website

2. First Impressions

When I first saw the thumbnail of this website, I thought it was a F1 dedicated website, which made me curious enough to look it up. Little did I know that what I would find would be much more impressive than that.

The website has a very unique and digital style that is able to catch people’s attention through the layout, as the composition has a messy but well done and organised touch, which can cause some curiosity.

Layout when hovering while playing one of the videos

3. Architecture Analysis

ORAGE is a website with more of a casual and informal tone of voice and some of the things that are worth highlighting from this website is definitely its composition, as none of its elements follow the convenient way of arrangement.

For starters, their navigation bar is right in the middle of the page, with the menu at the very front of it. Furthermore, the content of this navigation bar changes depending on the page that the user is navigating through. In some cases, this type of organisation would lead to a confusing navigation, but in this case, it doesn’t affect its natural navigation flow.

The reason for that is because of the organisation of the website, as each page only contains its designated parts. That is to say, on the home page the user will only find introductory short clips of their works, without giving any type of clue about the information that may go in the “about us” page or in “contact” one. Also, having the type of navigation bar that this website provides, really makes it easier for the user to go from one page to the other, and in an ordered way because of its dedicated-page theme.

Besides, each of these pages follows a different technique too. While the home and the about page use the one-page technique, the contact page is simply a smaller window that will appear on the users screen, and the pages that showcase their projects and the studio have an infinite layout that lets the user to scroll continually and have a quick view of everything that this studio is able to provide as a post production team.

Start of the infinite scrolling in the studio page





Interactive elements (pictures and navigation bar) when clicking on it

4. Visual Analysis

This page uses a much more minimalistic theme, by using only black and white for their brand’s colors. Nevertheless, because of their colorful works, the use of black and white creates the perfect balance between the brand and its works.

The main typography that they use is PP Supply Mono. This is a sans serif typography that simulates the typography that is normally used on futuristic films for computer programs, which creates a great cohesiveness with the technology-inspired elements that this website has. And apart from that, they use, for the page title, a bold sans serif typography on their backgrounds too, which also contributes to creating depth on the visual aspect of the website.

For their imagery, there aren’t many images outside of the videos that show their works. However, the simple background elements and texts do help to make the background look less empty, while also contributing to the construction of the aesthetic of the site, which matches the aesthetic of their works.

Contact popup when opened





Animated pictures that turn into videos when hovering

5. Interaction

When it comes to interaction, most of the elements on their website are interactive in a very creative way. Starting from the very bottom, there’s the contact page, which is a small popup element that appears in the middle of the website as an animated piece, as if someone was writing code.

Moreover, it’s the draggable elements. Firstly, that previously mentioned navigation bar, which is initially situated in the middle of the screen, has a little icon at the very front, which marks that the element is draggable, making it easier for the user to look at the content on each page without any type of interruption. What’s more, when the user drags it somewhere, the navigation bar will stay in that position through all the pages until it’s moved again.

Secondly, the user will find on the screen of the home page many small windows that will show the same video in a deconstructed way. Not only can the user click on this videos, which will make a popup video, with audio, to appear, but also, all these windows can be dragged to any part of the screen, while continuing to play the video, however, depending on where that window is located, it will show a section or another from that video that it keeps replaying.

Something similar happens on the project and studio pages, where the user will also find a lot of windows, but in this case, these will show a thumbnail that starts playing a video when it is hovered on. Again, all these videos can be played by clicking on them, and will appear as a popup.

Home page with interacive elements when clicking or dragging





Opened section of the navigation bar when playing a video

6. Usability & Accessibility

At first sight, the overall composition of the website might make it seem as if the navigation would be kind of messy. Nonetheless, the user experience is rather logical and smooth. This is all achieved through its easy access to the menu, and the minimalistic type of website that it is, as it only showcases the most important things of each page in a direct way.

In addition, the loading time doesn’t seem long, as it uses a loader animation which entertains the user, making it seem as if the loading time isn’t long. This loader is adapted into a simpler animation for the mobile version, where the layout seems more structured because of its reduced space.

About page

7. Excellence Criteria & Conclusion

CATEGORY CRITERIA
DESIGN 4/5☆ Its design possesses a futuristic vibe that creates a cohesive visual identity with their past projects, making the whole website look coordinated and balanced.
USABILITY 5/5☆ All the elements are easy to understand and to play around with. And it doesn’t include any unnecessary things that make the navigation harder than it should.
CREATIVITY 4/5☆ The simplicity of this website really brings out its creativity. Without many components, the website is still able to entertain the user through interactive elements.
CONTENT 4/5☆ The user is able to see and understand in a completely visual way the main purpose of the website clearly, because of the direct message that each page provides through its components and the name of the page.
CONCLUSION
4,25/5☆		 To sum up, this website is an innovative way of exhibiting a portfolio, design-wise, as it not only shows perfectly the style of the studio, but is able to make the user experience enjoyable and fun with interactions and videos.
