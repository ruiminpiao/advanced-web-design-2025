Award-Winning Sites' Analysis

Welcome! This is a small analysis of four different award-winning websites that I find interesting in one way or another.

Start reading

JIII ATELIER

Click here to go to the website

1. Introduction

The website of JIII ATELIER is a minimalistic portfolio website created by Yumi Nakaji for a production office that goes by the name of JIII ATELIER.

This site showcases what the website is, the studio's portfolio, but also, it promotes the services that the studio offers to its clients.

2. First Impressions

The first thing that made me take a look at this website was its color palette. I am usually very much attracted to anything that uses pastel colors, but in this website, it mixes it with a gradient, which I am not always a big fan of. However, in this case, the overall look of the website manages to bring me a strong calming feeling, which accentuates the desire to interact more with the website.

3. Architecture Analysis

The JIII ATELIER website is more formal than casual multiple sections and pages type of website, as the home page can be slowly scrolled to discover the rest of the content on the page. Which brings us to the menu, on the top right corner, the website provides the user a menu with the shape of a star inside a square, which opens up the different pages that are able to visit inside the website.

Regarding its cohesiveness, the website offers on the home page its main services, while also showcasing some of their previous work, while on the menu, the order varies a little bit, and after the home page, the most important page (following the hierarchy that is on the website) is the “about” page. This can be a little confusing, but it doesn’t affect the easy access that the user has to finding the different parts of the website.

Later on, we can also see some interesting things along the page, such as horizontal navigation where their services are provided. This is shown in rectangular UX cards, that are situated one behind the other in a horizontal carousel, which works by scrolling, or by clicking the pagination icons shown below those same UX cards. Besides this, there is also a section where instead of scrolling downwards, the elements come up while scrolling, but without the page going on on its length, much as if the screen was freezed, but not the elements of the page.

Moreover, the textual content of the website doesn’t contain any mistakes. However, it is kind of brief, making it harder to understand some of the content, as the website itself doesn’t have any type of storytelling that could give the user more clues of the paragraphs.





4. Visual Analysis

As it was mentioned before, the color palette uses different pastel colors, more specifically: white, pink, blue and purple, bringing out a softer approach of the brand.

For the typography, it mainly uses three. Two sans serif ones, which are Satoshi, used for most of the text and Noto Sans JP, used only for the text that is in Japanese; and a serif one that is Gall, which is used as an accent, for those words that he wants to give more importance.

Furthermore, the image style is mostly 3D illustration, which doesn’t really correlate with the way they are promoting the studio’s services. And also, there are some sections where the fixed elements overlap with the text, making it hard to read, Nevertheless, the overall experience of the website makes up for it, with little animations and a good layout that doesn’t look too overdone, but with enough space to let the user breathe through their scroll.

5. Interaction

When it comes to interaction, it is mostly easy and fun to scroll through the home page, as it has many 3D animations that makes the overall page much more immersive. For starters, the first part of the home page uses their logo as a 3D element that moves backwards the moment the scrolling starts, until one point, where it comes back again, until it disappears out of the viewport.

Also, there is a creative touch to the menu and the rectangular UX cards, as the cursor changes to a large, pixelated arrow, when in contact with the menu icon or the UX cards part. As well, when there are buttons for the user to look up more information about that section, the buttons change from a plus icon to a little star, and the text buttons have a tiny animation in their letters, in the same way as the arcade machines, where the different elements turn 360º one right after the other.

6. Usability & Accessibility

In general, it is easy to access the different parts of the website, as there isn’t much content for the user to get lost at some point. Nevertheless, the big UX cards in the “works” section do make it difficult to click on each card, as the button is very visible and it changes from a plus sign to a star, making it unclear for the user to know exactly whether they should click on the button or just anywhere on the UX card.

Regarding the loading times, the website doesn’t take much time to, but it could also be hidden with the text animations that are happening in the meantime. Also, it is adapted to smaller devices, by changing the size of the bigger UX cards, and the amount of elements that can be added to the viewport.

7. Conclusion

All in all, I believe that this website was aesthetically pleasing, and it worked well in general. It might have its little negative points, which may cause little mishaps on the user experience, but overall, it was easy and interesting.

8. Excellence Criteria

CATEGORY CRITERIA
DESIGN 3/5☆ The design gives a sense of cleanliness and relaxation with its minimalistic style, and the colors also give a feeling of a magic and sweet dream, making the first impression very enjoyable.
USABILITY 3/5☆ The website is easy to understand and to navigate, without many complications or elements that can cause misunderstandings.
CREATIVITY 3/5☆ Even though most of their animations and elements aren’t very innovative, I also feel as if the way they have used them brings a new perspective of how to use these types of things.
CONTENT 1/5☆ The user is able to see what the main point of the website is, but the use of only Japanese in some parts, and the little pieces of information can create some doubts about the information.
OVERALL SCORE: 2,5/5☆ The website is visually well done, even if some of its elements overlap in some parts, but the content may leave some questions to the user, making it hard to accomplish the site's main goal: to get clients for the studio.

PICTORIC

Click here to go to the website

1. Introduction

Founded in 2014, PICTORIC is a website made by a group of independent Ukrainian curators to build a creative circle for different illustrators and designers, make new projects come alive, and also, to organize exhibitions and workshops.

The main purpose of this website is not only to support illustrators and other creative people around the world, but to spread knowledge about Ukrainian culture and, to advertise the need of a higher importance of the visual language in the academic and social sector.

2. First Impressions

There’re actually two different elements that caught my eye at first: the illustration style and the color palette. I believe that the little star illustrations feels very amicable, and mixed with the vibrant colors that they have used, the site gives a really kind and soft vibe.

Overall, the website looks visually appealing and interesting with a first quick scroll through its home page, as there are many illustrations that are able to catch the user’s attention.

3. Architecture Analysis

The PICTORIC website is a multiple paged website that has a casual tone to its whole concept, with the use of cute illustrations that follow on their own a very subtle and progressive storytelling, showing a general idea of the creative process, which matches really well the concept of the website.

The menu on the website is already exposed on the right side of the navigation bar, where it appears with a flower, which could also be confused as an extension of the menu. However, the home page is organised so that it shows all the different pages that the website has, and that are stated on the menu, making it easy for the user to find each section, as each part has its own “more info” button.

Besides, without the use of any special techniques, the navigation begins by showing some illustration projects, and ending with the possible subscription for their illustration contest. Making it cohesive by creating a common storyline throughout the whole navigation. This is achieved by showing pictures of already existing artworks, and with the help of larger texts that explain what everything is in a clear and uninterrupted way, and also, some key words scattered in some parts of the website that help you understand their concept.





4. Visual Analysis

The website, which follows a cohesive layout with many interesting animations, is mainly composed by elements that use a vibrant yellow and blue, along with white and grey, to create a visual balance, and not to saturate the site with too much color, as the sections of the home page that showcase some illustrators’ works already contain too many colors, which can look overwhelming if it is overcombined with the blue and yellow used in the website’s color palette.

For the typography, the principal one is GTAmericaLCGV, a sans serif typography that brings out more of the casual tone of the website, and is used all along the platform for big titles, subtitles, additional information and text bodies. However, we can also observe that their logo uses a more geometric type of typography, which kind of simulates paper cut letters, and on some animations and details they use a more calligraphic typography that matches the illustration style of the site.

On the other hand, as it was mentioned previously, the image (illustration) style that this site possesses is friendly and organic, with a cute vibe to it. And it is this image style that brings the visual side of the website to match with its main message, to bring illustrators around the world together.

5. Interaction

The interactivity of this website is truly entertaining since the start of the home page. At the very top, on the navigation bar we can find an animated logo, where its letters rotate 360º one after the other, and a menu that has hovering effects, along with a little flower illustration that not only rotates on its axis while the user scrolls through the length of the home page, but it also shows an animated quote when it’s clicked on.

Then, on the home page there are some other interesting interactions. Starting from the hovering elements, there are two sections in which we can find some circles of different sizes, where the small ones may contain words or illustrations. Those circles move when hovered, and the ones at the top of the home page are even accessible, taking the user to a new page of the website. Also, in some sections, the cursor changes to a circumference that lets you drag the different elements in that part. Following that, there is a very compelling section, which is the one for their contest. On this part, the cursor will draw a thick line, which will slowly disappear, wherever it hovers, and this interaction is also applied to their boutique page, which is now empty.

The site may not use any sounds or 3D elements, but the combination of the existing interactions makes it creative and fun to see, as the user can play with many different parts of the website, instead of just scrolling through it.

6. Usability & Accessibility

The loading time is friendly, as the only moment that it takes “longer” to load is when the user first opens the website. Nevertheless, the loading time is clearly shown on the screen, making the wait more accessible and easy.

Regarding the user experience, the navigation follows a clear order that is easy to comprehend, without many things that can be truly confusing for the user. Except for the little flower icon on the navigation bar, which disappears on smaller devices where the responsiveness of the website changes the whole navigation bar and substitutes it for their logo and a hamburger menu icon.

7. Conclusion

In a nutshell, I find this website really pleasing to scroll through because of its many microinteractions with the user. Moreover, the style and purpose feels consistent throughout the totality of the site, which makes the whole experience much more enjoyable.

8. Excellence Criteria

CATEGORY CRITERIA
DESIGN 4/5☆ The overall design is aesthetically pleasing, bringing the user a happy feeling with its style and lively colours.
USABILITY 4/5☆ The navigation doesn’t give the user any confusing directions, making the whole process clear and entertaining with the included interactions.
CREATIVITY 5/5☆ Even though its simplicity, the site has found a way to create a balance between the designated style of the website and the artworks that have been presented to their community, giving place to a highly creative, but not oversaturated space.
CONTENT 4/5☆ The elements that appear are really cohesive with their respective texts, directing the user through the website’s content in a logical and direct way, which makes the user understand from the very beginning what the main purpose of this site is.
OVERALL SCORE: 2,5/5☆ The website is visually well done, even if some of its elements overlap in some parts, but the content may leave some questions to the user, making it hard to accomplish the site's main goal: to get clients for the studio.
