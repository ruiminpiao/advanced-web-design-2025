Award-Winning Sites' Analysis

Welcome! This is a small analysis of four different award-winning websites that I find interesting in one way or another.

Start reading

JIII ATELIER

Click here to go to the website

1. Introduction

The website of JIII ATELIER is a minimalistic portfolio website created by Yumi Nakaji for a production office that goes by the name of JIII ATELIER.

This site showcases what the website is, the studio's portfolio, but also, it promotes the services that the studio offers to its clients.

2. First Impressions

The first thing that made me take a look at this website was its color palette. I am usually very much attracted to anything that uses pastel colors, but in this website, it mixes it with a gradient, which I am not always a big fan of. However, in this case, the overall look of the website manages to bring me a strong calming feeling, which accentuates the desire to interact more with the website.

3. Architecture Analysis

The JIII ATELIER website is more formal than casual multiple sections and pages type of website, as the home page can be slowly scrolled to discover the rest of the content on the page. Which brings us to the menu, on the top right corner, the website provides the user a menu with the shape of a star inside a square, which opens up the different pages that are able to visit inside the website.

Regarding its cohesiveness, the website offers on the home page its main services, while also showcasing some of their previous work, while on the menu, the order varies a little bit, and after the home page, the most important page (following the hierarchy that is on the website) is the “about” page. This can be a little confusing, but it doesn’t affect the easy access that the user has to finding the different parts of the website.

Later on, we can also see some interesting things along the page, such as horizontal navigation where their services are provided. This is shown in rectangular UX cards, that are situated one behind the other in a horizontal carousel, which works by scrolling, or by clicking the pagination icons shown below those same UX cards. Besides this, there is also a section where instead of scrolling downwards, the elements come up while scrolling, but without the page going on on its length, much as if the screen was freezed, but not the elements of the page.

Moreover, the textual content of the website doesn’t contain any mistakes. However, it is kind of brief, making it harder to understand some of the content, as the website itself doesn’t have any type of storytelling that could give the user more clues of the paragraphs.





4. Visual Analysis

As it was mentioned before, the color palette uses different pastel colors, more specifically: white, pink, blue and purple, bringing out a softer approach of the brand.

For the typography, it mainly uses three. Two sans serif ones, which are Satoshi, used for most of the text and Noto Sans JP, used only for the text that is in Japanese; and a serif one that is Gall, which is used as an accent, for those words that he wants to give more importance.

Furthermore, the image style is mostly 3D illustration, which doesn’t really correlate with the way they are promoting the studio’s services. And also, there are some sections were the fixed elements overlap with the text, making it hard to read, Nevertheless, the overall experience of the website makes up for it, with little animations and a good layout that doesn’t look too overdone, but with enough space to let the user breathe through their scroll.

5. Interaction

When it comes to interaction, it is mostly easy and fun to scroll through the home page, as it has many 3D animations that makes the overall page much more immersive. For starters, the first part of the home page uses their logo as a 3D element that moves backwards the moment the scrolling starts, until one point, where it comes back again, until it disappears out of the viewport.

Also, there is a creative touch to the menu and the rectangular UX cards, as the cursor changes to a large, pixelated arrow, when in contact with the menu icon or the UX cards part. As well, when there are buttons for the user to look up more information about that section, the buttons change from a plus icon to a little star, and the text buttons have a tiny animation in their letters, in the same way as the arcade machines, where the different elements turn 360º one right after the other.

6. Usability & Accessibility

In general, it is easy to access the different parts of the website, as there isn’t much content for the user to get lost at some point. Nevertheless, the big UX cards in the “works” section do make it difficult to click on each card, as the button is very visible and it changes from a plus sign to a star, making it unclear for the user to know exactly whether they should click on the button or just anywhere on the UX card.

Regarding the loading times, the website doesn’t take much time to, but it could also be hidden with the text animations that are happening in the meantime. Also, it is adapted to smaller devices, by changing the size of the bigger UX cards, and the amount of elements that can be added to the viewport.

7. Conclusion

All in all, I believe that this website was aesthetically pleasing, and it worked well in general. It might have its little negative points, which may cause little mishaps on the user experience, but overall, it was easy and interesting.

8. Excellence Criteria

CATEGORY CRITERIA
DESIGN 3/5☆ The design gives a sense of cleanliness and relaxation with its minimalistic style, and the colors also give a feeling of a magic and sweet dream, making the first impression very enjoyable.
USABILITY 3/5☆ The website is easy to understand and to navigate, without many complications or elements that can cause misunderstandings.
CREATIVITY 3/5☆ Even though most of their animations and elements aren’t very innovative, I also feel as if the way they have used them brings a new perspective of how to use these types of things.
CONTENT 1/5☆ The user is able to see what the main point of the website is, but the use of only Japanese in some parts, and the little pieces of information can create some doubts about the information.
OVERALL SCORE: 2,5/5☆ The website is visually well done, even if some of its elements overlap in some parts, but the content may leave some questions to the user, making it hard to accomplish the site's main goal: to get clients for the studio.
